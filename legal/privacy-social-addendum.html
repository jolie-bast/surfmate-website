3.7 Push notifications

If you enable push notifications, we store and process a device push token or comparable technical identifier (e.g. from your operating system’s push service) so notifications can be delivered to your device. This token is personal data because it may be linked to your user account. Depending on your notification settings, push notifications may relate to spotchecks, direct messages, comments on your surf sessions, or likes on your session comments. Enabling notifications is voluntary via system settings or the notification settings screen in the App.

3.11 Mates feed, session comments, likes, and mate tagging

Surfmate includes social features in addition to your personal session log:

  • Mates feed: When you follow other users, their public surf sessions (from public spots, marked visible to others) may appear in your mates feed. Users who follow you may see your public sessions in the same way, subject to blocking settings.
  • Session comments and likes: Eligible users may post short text comments on surf sessions and like comments. We store comment text, like records, timestamps, and author profile references. This data is shown to users who can access the session according to visibility rules (including spot co-owners where applicable).
  • In-app notifications: We may create in-app notifications when someone comments on your session or likes your comment.
  • Mate tagging: When a user logs a session and tags you as having surfed with them, we process your user ID to send you a direct message about the session, allow you to view that session in the App, and—where the session uses a private spot owned by the person who logged it—may grant you co-access to that private spot in your session log so you can view or copy the session. Tagging is initiated by the user who logged the session; you can use block and other safety features if you do not want interaction with a particular user.

Legal bases: providing features you use (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR); abuse prevention and community safety (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR); consent where required for optional push notifications (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR).

Reportable content types include chat messages, spotchecks, surf sessions, session comments, user profiles, avatar media, and spots. When content is removed following a report, we notify the affected user with a brief reason where appropriate and provide an appeal option in the App.
